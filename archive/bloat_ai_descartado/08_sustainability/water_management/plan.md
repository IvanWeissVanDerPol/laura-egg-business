## 4. Water Quality Standards

### Required Parameters

| Parameter | Acceptable | Critical | Test Frequency |
|-----------|------------|----------|----------------|
| pH | 6.5-7.5 | <6 or >8 | Monthly |
| Bacteria (E. coli) | 0 CFU/100ml | Any | Quarterly |
| Hardness | 60-180 mg/L | <30 or >300 | Quarterly |
| Nitrate | <50 mg/L | >100 | Annually |
| Iron | <0.3 mg/L | >1.0 | Annually |
| Total Dissolved Solids | <500 mg/L | >1000 | Annually |

### Testing Schedule
- [ ] Monthly: pH, chlorine
- [ ] Quarterly: Full bacteriological
- [ ] Annually: Comprehensive chemical analysis

### Water Treatment Options

| Issue | Treatment | Cost |
|-------|-----------|------|
| High bacteria | Chlorination | Low |
| Low pH | Acidification | Low |
| High iron | Filtration | Medium |
| Hard water | Softener | Medium |
| High nitrate | RO system | High |

## 7. Backup Systems

### Emergency Water Plan

| Scenario | Duration | Water Needed | Backup Source |
|----------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Pump failure | 1-3 days | 450-900 L | Municipal or stored |
| Drought | Weeks | Reduced ration | Truck delivery |
| Contamination | Unknown | Clean source | Bottled water |

### Storage Requirements

| Capacity | Days of Supply | Recommended |
|----------|----------------|-------------|
| 1,000 birds | 3 days | 2,000 L minimum |
| 1,000 birds | 1 week | 5,000 L |
| 3,000 birds | 3 days | 6,000 L |
| 3,000 birds | 1 week | 15,000 L |

### Emergency Rationing Protocol
1. Assess remaining water
2. Calculate daily minimum (100ml/bird)
3. Implement nipple-only (no open water)
4. Prioritize drinking over cleaning
5. Source emergency supply immediately
6. Communicate with workers
