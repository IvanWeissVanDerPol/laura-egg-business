# MAG and Government Financing Programs for Agricultural Producers in Paraguay

## 1. Overview of Agricultural Financing in Paraguay

### Key Institutions
| Institution | Focus | Products |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| MAG | Ministry of Agriculture | Subsidized programs, technical assistance |
| CAH | Agricultural Habilitation Credit | Small to medium producers |
| BNF | Banco Nacional de Fomenta | Agricultural loans, development |
| ITAIPU | Itaipu Binacional | Rural development grants |
| FAO | UN Food Agency | Technical cooperation |

## 2. Crédito Agrícola de Habilitación (CAH)

### Purpose
Provide credit and technical assistance to small and medium agricultural producers who lack access to traditional banking.

### Eligibility Requirements
- [ ] Active in agricultural production
- [ ] Maximum 50 hectares of productive land
- [ ] Not exceeding G. 500,000,000 in annual sales
- [ ] Cannot have CAH loans in default
- [ ] Must complete basic training program

### Loan Products

#### Production Credit
- **Amount**: G. 10,000,000 - G. 200,000,000
- **Term**: 1-3 years
- **Interest Rate**: 8-12% annual (subsidized)
- **Purpose**: Seeds, fertilizers, labor, equipment

#### Investment Credit
- **Amount**: G. 50,000,000 - G. 500,000,000
- **Term**: 3-10 years
- **Interest Rate**: 10-15% annual
- **Purpose**: Infrastructure, machinery, expansion

#### Working Capital
- **Amount**: G. 5,000,000 - G. 100,000,000
- **Term**: Up to 18 months
- **Interest Rate**: 8-12% annual
- **Purpose**: Seasonal operating costs

### Application Process
1. **Registration**: Register with CAH in your department
2. **Training**: Complete 20-hour basic agricultural course
3. **Project**: Submit production/business plan
4. **Evaluation**: CAH technical staff visit
5. **Approval**: Regional committee approval
6. **Disbursement**: Funds transferred to supplier or account

### Required Documentation
- [ ] CEDULA (ID) copies
- [ ] RUC certificate
- [ ] Land title or lease agreement
- [ ] Production plan with budget
- [ ] References from community leaders
- [ ] Environmental self-assessment form

## 3. Banco Nacional de Fomento (BNF)

### Agricultural Loan Programs

#### Crédito Agrícola BNF
- **Amount**: G. 50,000,000 - G. 2,000,000,000
- **Term**: 1-15 years
- **Interest Rate**: 12-18% annual
- **Collateral**: Land, equipment, or guarantees

#### Programa de Desarrollo Rural
- **Amount**: Up to G. 3,000,000,000
- **Term**: 5-20 years
- **Interest Rate**: 8-12% annual (subsidized)
- **Purpose**: Infrastructure, equipment, working capital

#### Lending Requirements
- Sound business plan
- Collateral or guarantee
- Credit history check
- Land ownership or long-term lease
- Technical viability assessment

### BNF Agricultural Products
| Product | Amount | Term | Rate |
|---------|--------|------|------|
| Avicultura | G. 50M-500M | 3-7 years | 12-15% |
| Granjas Integrales | G. 100M-1B | 5-10 years | 10-14% |
| Capital de Trabajo | G. 20M-200M | 1-2 years | 14-18% |

## 4. Ministerio de Agricultura y Ganadería (MAG) Programs

### 4.1 Programa de Desarrollo Productivo Rural (PDPR)

#### Objectives
- Increase agricultural productivity
- Improve rural quality of life
- Strengthen food security
- Promote sustainable practices

#### Components
1. **Technical Assistance**: Free extension services
2. **Direct Grants**: For productive investments
3. **Training**: Farmer field schools
4. **Market Access**: Value chain development

#### Eligibility
- Small and medium producers
- Registered with MAG
- Active in production
- Willing to adopt improved practices

#### Application
- Contact local MAG office
- Submit production profile
- Participate in planning workshop
- Receive technical visit
- Project approval and implementation

### 4.2 Plan de Granjas Avícolas

#### Focus Areas
- Layer hen production
- Broiler production
- Small-scale integration

#### Available Support
- Technical training
- Equipment subsidies (up to 30%)
- Breed improvement programs
- Biosecurity assistance

### 4.3 MAG Input Programs

#### Distribution Programs
- **Semillas**: Seed distribution programs
- **Fertilizantes**: Fertilizer subsidies
- **Veterinarios**: Vaccination campaigns
- **Genética**: Breed improvement

#### Application Process
- Register with local MAG office
- Attend planning meetings
- Submit production plan
- Receive inputs at subsidized prices

## 5. ITAIPU Binacional Programs

### Rural Development Grants

#### FONAVIPA (Fondo de Valores de Itaipu para Productores)
- Grants for rural producers
- Focus on family agriculture
- Amount: G. 5,000,000 - G. 50,000,000

#### Requisitos ITAIPU
- Reside in beneficiary zone
- Active agricultural production
- No land conflicts
- Environmental compliance
- Community participation

### Energy for Agriculture
- Rural electrification programs
- Solar panel subsidies for farms
- Irrigation system support

## 9. Application Tips

### Before Applying
1. **Organize Documentation**: Land title, RUC, ID, production records
2. **Clear Debts**: Resolve any existing loan defaults
3. **Update Knowledge**: Know your production costs and projections
4. **Prepare Business Plan**: Even simple plans help
5. **Visit Offices**: Build relationships with credit officers

### Common Reasons for Rejection
- Incomplete documentation
- Poor credit history
- Unrealistic projections
- Land ownership disputes
- Environmental violations
- Lack of technical knowledge

### How to Improve Approval Chances
1. Start with CAH for first loans
2. Build credit history gradually
3. Join producer cooperatives
4. Get technical training certificates
5. Maintain good farm records
6. Show environmental compliance

## 10. Recommended Action Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)
- [ ] Register with MAG as producer
- [ ] Complete CAH basic training
- [ ] Organize all land documentation
- [ ] Open dedicated business bank account
- [ ] Begin keeping production records

### Phase 2: First Credit (Month 3-4)
- [ ] Apply to CAH for first production credit
- [ ] Start with small amount (G. 10-20M)
- [ ] Use funds for planned improvements
- [ ] Make payments on time
- [ ] Build credit history

### Phase 3: Expansion (Month 6-12)
- [ ] Evaluate first credit results
- [ ] Apply for larger investment credit
- [ ] Consider BNF for expansion capital
- [ ] Explore MAG technical programs
- [ ] Investigate cooperative membership

### Phase 4: Growth (Year 2+)
- [ ] Multiple credit relationships
- [ ] Access to larger BNF investments
- [ ] Participate in value chain programs
- [ ] Consider export market development
- [ ] Professional farm management
