# Marketing Pricing Strategy

## 1. Price Psychology

### Key Principles

| Principle | Application | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Charm pricing | End in 9 | Gs. 14,900 vs 15,000 |
| Bundle pricing | Package deals | 10 trays = discount |
| Anchor pricing | Show original | "Was 18,000, now 15,000" |
| Premium positioning | Quality = higher | Gs. 18,000 vs 14,000 |

### Psychological Pricing Tips

- Gs. 14,900 feels cheaper than Gs. 15,000
- "Premium" justifies higher prices
- Round numbers for invoices/bulk
- Odd numbers for retail/small quantities

## 5. Promotional Pricing

### Types of Promotions

| Type | Frequency | Discount | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|----------|
| New customer | Once | 15% | First order |
| Volume | Monthly | 5-10% | Limited |
| Seasonal | Quarterly | 5% | 1-2 weeks |
| Off-size | As available | 20% | Until sold |
| Loyalty | Annual | 10% | After 6 months |

### Promotion Calendar

| Month | Promotion | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| January | New Year sale (5%) | Post-holiday |
| March | Anniversary (10%) | Brand milestone |
| June | Winter comfort (5%) | Higher demand |
| September | Back to business | New school year |
| December | Holiday bulk (10%) | Baking season |

### Promotion Rules

1. **Never below cost** - Gs. 11,000 minimum
2. **Communicate value** - "Premium eggs on sale"
3. **Limit duration** - Creates urgency
4. **Track results** - Did it work?
5. **Don't overdo** - Devalues brand

## 6. Dynamic Pricing

### Factors That Affect Price

| Factor | Direction | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Feed cost increase | Up | +5% for every 15% feed increase |
| Holiday demand | Up | +10% December |
| Competition price war | Hold | Quality emphasis |
| New competitor | Hold/lower | 5% if needed |
| High demand | Up | +5-10% |
| Low supply | Up | +10-15% |
| Customer loyalty | Maintain | Reward with service |

### Price Adjustment Process

1. Identify trigger factor
2. Calculate cost impact
3. Determine price adjustment
4. Prepare customer communication
5. Implement with notice
6. Monitor response

## 7. Price Communication

### How to Communicate Prices

**DO:**
- Clear, written price lists
- Consistent pricing
- Timely updates
- Explain value, not just price
- Train sales staff

**DON'T:**
- Hidden fees
- Surprise increases
- Unclear terms
- Negotiate against yourself
- Apologize for prices

### Price Announcement Template

```
Hola [Customer],

Nuevos precios a partir de [Date]:

[Product] - Gs. [Price]

Razón: [Explanation - e.g., "incremento en costos de alimentación"]

Gracias por su comprensión y confianza.
```

## 9. Pricing Decision Tree

```
Is this a new customer?
├─ Yes → Offer new customer discount (15%)
└─ No → Continue...

Is this high volume (>100 doz)?
├─ Yes → Apply volume discount
└─ No → Standard pricing

Is this a premium customer?
├─ Yes → Maintain/raise price
└─ No → Standard pricing

Is our costs higher than last month?
├─ Yes → Can we absorb? → Price increase?
└─ No → Standard pricing

Is competitor priced lower?
├─ Yes → Emphasize quality, hold price
└─ No → Opportunity to maintain

Does price meet minimum floor?
├─ Yes → Proceed
└─ No → Decline or renegotiate terms
```
