# Paraguay Tax Guide for Agricultural Businesses

## 1. Paraguay Tax System Overview

### Key Tax Authorities
| Authority | Role |
|-----------|------|
| SET (Subsecretaría de Estado de Tributación) | Federal tax collection |
| Hacienda | National budget and fiscal policy |
| Municipalidades | Local taxes |
| IPS | Social security contributions |

### Fiscal Year
- Calendar year (January 1 - December 31)
- Annual declarations due by March 31 of following year

## 2. Main Business Taxes

### 2.1 Income Tax - IRAE (Impuesto a la Renta de Actividades Económicas)

**Rate**: 10% on net taxable income

**Agricultural Specifics**:
- Income from agricultural production has special treatment
- Land that remains idle may be subject to additional taxation
- Forestry activities have different rates

**Calculation**:
```
Net Taxable Income = Gross Income - Deductible Expenses
Tax = Net Taxable Income × 10%
```

**Deductible Expenses** (must be documented):
- Operating costs (feed, labor, utilities)
- Depreciation of equipment and buildings
- Veterinary expenses
- Interest on agricultural loans
- Insurance premiums
- Property taxes paid

### 2.2 Value Added Tax - IVA

**Standard Rate**: 10%

**Agricultural Exemptions**:
- Sale of unprocessed agricultural products is VAT-exempt
- This includes: eggs in shell, live animals, unprocessed milk
- Processed products (cooked, packaged with additives) are subject to IVA

**IVA Credit System**:
- Agricultural producers can credit IVA paid on inputs
- Inputs for primary production: feed, veterinary products, equipment
- Must have fiscal invoices (facturas) to claim credits

**Key IVA Rules**:
- Register for IVA if annual sales exceed G. 36,000,000 (~$5,000 USD)
- Issue fiscal invoices for all sales
- Monthly IVA declarations (form 110)
- IVA rate on agricultural inputs: 10%

### 2.3 Single Tax on Flush-Old Cattle - IMAGRO

**Rate**: 10% on gross agricultural income

**Application**:
- Alternative to IRAE for some agricultural activities
- Can be chosen instead of IRAE
- Most common for cattle, applicable to poultry

**Election**:
- Must elect at beginning of fiscal year
- Cannot change mid-year
- Consult accountant before election

## 3. Property Taxes

### 3.1 Immovable Property Tax - ICP

**Rate**: 0.5% - 1% on cadastral value

**Agricultural Land**:
- Special assessment for agricultural use
- Idle land penalty: 2% additional
- Forest reserves may have exemptions

**Exemptions**:
- First G. 75,000,000 of cadastral value
- Land used for agricultural production
- Cooperative member properties

### 3.2 Vehicle Tax

**Agricultural Vehicles**:
- Tractors: 0% or reduced rate
- Trucks for agricultural transport: 1%
- Farm vehicles: Check with municipality

## 4. Social Security - IPS

### Employer Contributions

**Rate**: 16.5% of gross salary

**Breakdown**:
| Contribution | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Health | 9% |
| Pension | 6% |
| Labor Risk | 1.5% |

### Employee Contributions
- 9% of salary (typically 9% for health + pension)
- Withheld by employer

### Agricultural Worker Considerations
- Seasonal workers: Calculate prorated contributions
- Day workers: Minimum wage basis
- Family workers: Check IPS requirements

## 5. Municipal Taxes

### Patent Registration
- Annual business license fee
- Based on capital and activity type
- Rates vary by municipality
- Due by March 31 each year

### Municipal Fee on Gross Revenue
- Rate: 0.5% - 1.5% depending on municipality
- Applied to gross revenue from municipal area
- Deductible from national income tax

### Other Municipal Fees
- Property verification fees
- Land use permits
- Construction permits
- Waste disposal fees

## 6. Agricultural Sector Benefits

### 6.1 Law 6380/2019 - Agricultural Promotion
- Income tax exemptions for new agricultural investments
- Import duty reductions on agricultural equipment
- Simplified registration procedures

### 6.2 Law 5542/2015 - Tax Stability
- Legal stability contracts for large investments
- Fixed tax rates for 5-10 years
- Requires investment over $5,000,000 USD

### 6.3 Cooperative Benefits
- Income tax exemption on member transactions
- Reduced municipal taxes
- Access to special credit lines

### 6.4 Export Incentives
- Drawback system for exporters
- VAT refund on exported goods
- Export tax exemptions

## 7. Record Keeping Requirements

### Required Documents
- [ ] Fiscal invoices (facturas) for all purchases
- [ ] Electronic invoices (e-facturas)
- [ ] Bank statements
- [ ] Payroll records
- [ ] Inventory records
- [ ] Asset depreciation schedules
- [ ] Agricultural activity logs

### Retention Period
- **Minimum**: 5 years for tax purposes
- **Recommended**: 10 years for complete records
- **Legal disputes**: Keep until resolved

### Digital Requirements
- Electronic invoicing mandatory for most businesses
- Digital tax returns through SET portal
- Online payment systems available

## 10. Tips for Tax Efficiency

### Legal Deductions
1. **Document Everything**: Keep all receipts and invoices
2. **Separate Accounts**: Business and personal finances
3. **Asset Depreciation**: Maximize depreciation schedules
4. **Invest in Equipment**: Agricultural equipment has favorable treatment
5. **Insurance**: Deductible business insurance premiums

### Avoid Common Mistakes
1. Don't mix personal and business expenses
2. Don't delay invoice collection
3. Don't skip quarterly estimates
4. Don't ignore municipal obligations
5. Don't forget IPS contributions

### Tax Planning Strategy
- Review annually before fiscal year ends
- Plan major purchases for tax efficiency
- Consider cooperative membership
- Evaluate IMAGRO vs IRAE election
- Plan for seasonal cash flow
