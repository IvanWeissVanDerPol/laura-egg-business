## 2. Technical Skills Training

### Module 1: Feeding Management

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand nutritional needs
- Operate feeding equipment
- Identify feeding problems
- Calculate feed amounts

**Content:**

```
FEEDING BASICS

Why feeding matters:
- 70% of production cost
- Directly affects egg quality
- Bird health depends on nutrition

Daily feeding schedule:
- Morning: 6:00 AM (40% of daily)
- Afternoon: 2:00 PM (30% of daily)
- Evening: 6:00 PM (30% of daily)

Signs of feeding problems:
- Feed left in trough
- Birds fighting over food
- Weight loss in birds
- Decreased production

Amount per bird per day:
- Layers: 110-130g
- Adjust based on consumption
```

**Practical Assessment:**
- [ ] Correctly measures feed
- [ ] Distributes evenly
- [ ] Identifies problems
- [ ] Records consumption

### Module 2: Egg Collection

**Learning Objectives:**
- Collect eggs properly
- Handle eggs carefully
- Identify quality issues
- Use grading equipment

**Content:**

```
EGG COLLECTION BEST PRACTICES

Collection times:
- Morning: 8:00-10:00 AM
- Afternoon: 2:00-4:00 PM
- Evening: 6:00-7:00 PM

Proper technique:
1. Approach nests calmly
2. Pick up eggs by base (large end)
3. Place in clean tray
4. Don't stack too high
5. Move to cooler within 1 hour

What to look for:
- Cracks: Discard
- Dirt: Clean or discard
- Misshapen: Note, may sell
- Blood spots: Discard

Quality check:
- Sample 30 eggs daily
- Check for cracks
- Verify grading accuracy
```

**Practical Assessment:**
- [ ] Collects without breaking
- [ ] Identifies problems correctly
- [ ] Handles hygienically
- [ ] Completes collection on time

### Module 3: Health Monitoring

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize normal vs. abnormal
- Identify sick birds
- Report health concerns
- Maintain health records

**Content:**

```
HEALTH MONITORING

Normal bird behavior:
- Active during day
- Eating and drinking
- Normal droppings (brown, firm)
- Good feather condition
- Consistent production

Warning signs:
- Lethargy, not moving
- Huddling together
- Pale comb/wattles
- Loss of appetite
- Respiratory sounds
- Watery droppings
- Decreased production

Daily health check:
1. Watch birds during feeding
2. Check for dead birds
3. Note any unusual behavior
4. Check droppings
5. Monitor water consumption

Action on concerns:
1. Document what you see
2. Isolate sick bird if possible
3. Tell manager immediately
4. Don't medicate without guidance
```

**Practical Assessment:**
- [ ] Identifies normal behavior
- [ ] Recognizes warning signs
- [ ] Documents observations
- [ ] Reports appropriately

### Module 4: Biosecurity

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand disease risks
- Follow biosecurity rules
- Use foot baths properly
- Prevent contamination

**Content:**

```
BIOSECURITY FUNDAMENTALS

Why biosecurity matters:
- Prevents disease
- Protects our birds
- Protects our business
- Keeps food safe

Key rules:
1. WASH HANDS before entering barn
2. Use FOOT BATHS at entry points
3. Wear designated CLOTHES/SHOES
4. No visitors without permission
5. No contact with other poultry
6. Report any concerns

Foot bath procedure:
1. Remove solid debris from shoes
2. Step into foot bath
3. Wait 30 seconds
4. Scrub shoes
5. Step out onto clean area

Signs of biosecurity breach:
- Dead birds
- Sick birds
- Visitor without following protocol
- Equipment shared with other farms
```

**Practical Assessment:**
- [ ] Demonstrates hand washing
- [ ] Uses foot bath correctly
- [ ] Follows visitor protocol
- [ ] Explains why rules matter

## 3. Safety Training

### Module 5: Farm Safety

**Topics:**
1. Chemical handling
2. Equipment safety
3. Electrical safety
4. Fire prevention
5. First aid basics

**Safety Rules:**

```
FARM SAFETY ESSENTIALS

Chemicals:
- Store separately from food/birds
- Use PPE (gloves, mask)
- Follow label instructions
- Know emergency procedures

Equipment:
- Turn off before maintenance
- Never reach into moving parts
- Report damage immediately
- Use only as trained

Electrical:
- No water near electricity
- Report frayed wires
- Don't overload outlets
- Keep cords organized

Fire:
- Know fire extinguisher location
- Keep exits clear
- No open flames
- Report fire hazards
```
