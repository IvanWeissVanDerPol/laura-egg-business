# IPSAN Requirements for Egg Production in Paraguay

> **Note:** IPSAN (Instituto Paraguayo de Tecnología Agraria) was absorbed into SENACSA (Servicio Nacional de Calidad y Salud Animal) for animal products. This document covers the general regulatory framework for egg production quality standards in Paraguay.

## 1. Overview

### SENACSA (Servicio Nacional de Calidad y Salud Animal)
- Primary authority for animal health and quality in Paraguay
- Oversees egg production facilities and standards
- Coordinates with INTN for product standardization

### Key Regulatory Bodies
| Authority | Role |
|-----------|------|
| SENACSA | Animal health, farm inspections |
| INTN | Product standards, quality certification |
| SENAVE | Agricultural inputs, pesticides |
| MSPyBS | Food safety for human consumption |

## 2. Egg Quality Standards

### Grade Classifications
| Grade | Shell Quality | Yolk | Albumen | Air Cell |
|-------|---------------|------|---------|----------|
| AA | Clean, unbroken | Bright, firm | Thick, stands high | 1/8" max |
| A | Clean, unbroken | Bright, firm | Thick, stands | 3/16" max |
| B | Clean to slightly stained | May be slightly flattened | Small amount of thin white | 3/8" max |

### Weight Classes (grams)
| Size | Minimum Weight |
|------|---------------|
| Jumbo | 70g |
| Extra Large | 63g |
| Large | 56g |
| Medium | 49g |
| Small | 42g |
| Peewee | 35g |

## 3. Facility Requirements

### Farm Infrastructure
- [ ] Concrete or paved floor in production areas
- [ ] Proper drainage system
- [ ] Adequate ventilation (natural or mechanical)
- [ ] Lighting: 14-16 hours daily
- [ ] Temperature control (18-24°C optimal)
- [ ] Separate areas for: reception, storage, packaging, office

### Biosecurity Measures
- [ ] Foot baths at entry points
- [ ] Hand washing stations
- [ ] Pest control program
- [ ] Wild bird/rodent barriers
- [ ] Worker changing areas
- [ ] Vehicle disinfection point

### Hygiene Requirements
- [ ] Daily egg collection (minimum 2x)
- [ ] Cleaning and disinfection schedule
- [ ] Manure management system
- [ ] Dead bird composting/disposal area
- [ ] Potable water supply certified

## 4. Egg Handling Standards

### Collection & Sorting
1. Collect eggs minimum twice daily
2. Handle gently to prevent cracks
3. Sort by size and quality immediately
4. Discard dirty, cracked, or abnormal eggs
5. Never wash eggs with cold water (causes condensation)

### Storage Requirements
| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Temperature | 12-15°C |
| Humidity | 70-80% |
| Maximum Storage | 21 days (retail) |
| Pallet height | Max 5 trays |

### Packaging Standards
- Clean, sanitized egg trays
- Proper labeling with:
  - Farm name and address
  - Production date
  - Expiration date
  - Grade and size
  - SENACSA registry number

## 5. Mandatory Documentation

### Farm Records
- [ ] Daily egg production log
- [ ] Feed consumption records
- [ ] Mortality records
- [ ] Vaccination schedule and administration
- [ ] Medication log (withdrawal periods)
- [ ] Visitor log
- [ ] Cleaning and disinfection log
- [ ] Water quality tests

### Required Registrations
- [ ] SENACSA producer registration
- [ ] RUC (Registro Único del Contribuyente)
- [ ] Municipal operating license
- [ ] Environmental permits (MADES)

## 6. Inspection Process

### Pre-Inspection Checklist
- [ ] All records up to date
- [ ] Facilities clean and organized
- [ ] Pest control documentation ready
- [ ] Water test results available
- [ ] Feed supplier certifications available
- [ ] Worker health records current

### Common Inspection Areas
1. **Biosecurity**: Entry protocols, foot baths, signage
2. **Housing**: Space per bird, ventilation, lighting
3. **Egg Handling**: Collection frequency, sorting, storage
4. **Records**: Completeness and accuracy
5. **Waste Management**: Manure and dead bird handling
6. **Food Safety**: Traceability, no unauthorized substances

## 7. Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Minor Violations
- Fines: 1-5 Gs. 10,000,000 (~$1,400-7,000 USD)
- Corrective action required within 30 days
- Possible temporary suspension of certification

### Major Violations
- Fines: 5-20 Gs. 10,000,000 (~$7,000-28,000 USD)
- Closure of facility until compliance
- Product seizure and destruction
- Criminal charges for food safety violations

### Critical Violations
- Operating without registration
- Presence of prohibited substances
- Disease outbreak cover-up
- Immediate facility shutdown
- Legal prosecution

## 8. Best Practices for Compliance

### Daily Operations
1. **Morning**: Check facilities, collect eggs, record production
2. **Midday**: Monitor feed/water, check bird health
3. **Afternoon**: Collect eggs, clean collection areas
4. **End of day**: Update all records, secure facility

### Quality Assurance Program
- Weekly internal audits
- Monthly third-party inspections
- Quarterly water quality testing
- Annual equipment calibration
- Continuous staff training

### Recommended Certifications
- BPA (Buenas Prácticas Agrícolas)
- ISO 22000 (Food Safety)
- HACCP certification for processing
