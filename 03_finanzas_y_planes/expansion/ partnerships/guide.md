# Strategic Partnerships Guide

## 1. Partnership Types

### Partnership Categories

| Type | Example | Benefit | Commitment |
|------|---------|---------|------------|
| **Suppliers** | Feed, equipment | Better prices | Regular orders |
| **Customers** | Supermarkets, restaurants | Volume sales | Contracts |
| **Producers** | Other farms | Shared services | Variable |
| **Institutions** | MAG, SENACSA | Support, credibility | Compliance |
| **Financial** | Banks, cooperatives | Capital access | Terms |
| **Technical** | Vets, consultants | Expertise | Retainers |

## 2. Supplier Partnerships

### Feed Suppliers

**Why Partner:**
- Secure supply
- Price stability
- Technical support
- Credit access

**Partnership Elements:**

| Element | Standard | Partnership |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Order frequency | Per-order | Weekly schedule |
| Price | Market | Fixed 3-6 months |
| Credit terms | Cash/7 days | 15-30 days |
| Delivery | Extra cost | Free on orders >X |
| Support | Basic | Technical advisory |

**How to Approach:**
1. Start as regular customer
2. Build 3-6 month relationship
3. Propose formal agreement
4. Negotiate partnership terms
5. Review quarterly

### Equipment Suppliers

**Partnership Benefits:**
- Priority service
- Extended warranties
- Volume discounts
- Early access to new products
- Training on equipment

**Partnership Activities:**
- Quarterly review meetings
- Equipment maintenance planning
- Feedback on products
- Joint problem-solving

## 3. Customer Partnerships

### Supermarket Chains

**Partnership Stages:**

| Stage | Focus | Commitment |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Trial | Test quality | 30 days |
| Onboarding | Listing process | 3 months |
| Evaluation | 3-month review | - |
| Partnership | Ongoing relationship | Annual |

**Partnership Requirements:**
- [ ] Consistent quality
- [ ] Reliable delivery
- [ ] Marketing support
- [ ] Competitive pricing
- [ ] Data sharing (sales)

**Partnership Benefits:**
- Preferred shelf placement
- Promotional support
- Longer payment terms
- Volume commitments
- Brand visibility

### Restaurant/Hotel Partners

**Types of Partnerships:**

| Type | Commitment | Benefits |
|------|------------|----------|
| Preferred Supplier | Exclusivity in category | Guaranteed volume |
| Contract Partner | 6-12 month contract | Fixed pricing |
| Seasonal Partner | Peak season only | Flexibility |
| Occasional Partner | As-needed orders | No commitment |

**How to Build:**
1. Deliver exceptional quality
2. Be reliable, always on time
3. Communicate proactively
4. Offer volume discounts
5. Request formal status

## 7. Partnership Development Process

### Step 1: Identify Opportunities

**Questions to Ask:**
- What do we need?
- Who can provide it?
- What's in it for them?
- What's the commitment?

### Step 2: Approach

**Initial Contact:**
```
Hola [Name], soy [Owner] de Granja Cabral.

He followed your business and see alignment with 
how we operate. I'm interested in discussing how 
we might work together more closely.

Would you have 30 minutes to explore a potential 
partnership?
```

### Step 3: Negotiate

**Key Terms to Define:**
- Duration
- Exclusivity
- Pricing/discounts
- Volume commitments
- Payment terms
- Termination clauses
- Quality standards

### Step 4: Formalize

**Document Everything:**
- Written agreements
- Signed contracts
- Clear expectations
- Performance metrics
- Review schedule

### Step 5: Maintain

**Partnership Health:**
- Quarterly reviews
- Open communication
- Address issues quickly
- Celebrate successes
- Add value to relationship

## 8. Partnership Evaluation

| [Name] | High | Medium | Good | Active |

### Review Questions

Monthly:
- Are we meeting commitments?
- Are they meeting theirs?
- Any issues to address?

Quarterly:
- Is partnership delivering value?
- Should terms be adjusted?
- Any new opportunities?

Annually:
- Full partnership review
- Renewal negotiations
- Strategic alignment

## 9. Partnership Pitfalls

### Common Mistakes

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Verbal agreements | Always document |
| Unclear terms | Be specific in writing |
| One-sided value | Ensure mutual benefit |
| Ignoring problems | Address early |
| No exit plan | Define termination |

### Warning Signs

- Unresponsive to communication
- Consistently missing commitments
- Quality declining
- Communication breakdown
- One party pulling away
