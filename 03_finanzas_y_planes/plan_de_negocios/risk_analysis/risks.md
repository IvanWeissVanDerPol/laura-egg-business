## 3. Critical Risks Deep Dive

### 3.1 Disease Outbreak

**Description:** Avian influenza or other disease wiping out flock

**Warning Signs:**
- Decreased egg production
- Unusual mortality
- Sick birds
- Neighbor farm outbreaks

**Prevention Protocol:**
- [ ] Strict biosecurity at entry
- [ ] Foot baths at all entrances
- [ ] No outside visitors without 48hr quarantine
- [ ] Regular vaccination program
- [ ] Rodent and pest control
- [ ] Clean equipment protocol
- [ ]隔离 new birds for 2 weeks

**Response Plan:**
1. Isolate affected birds immediately
2. Contact SENACSA (animal health)
3. Implement quarantine protocols
4. Assess mortality and impact
5. Plan restocking if needed
6. Review and improve biosecurity

**Insurance:** Consider poultry insurance with SENACSA

### 3.2 Feed Cost Volatility

**Description:** Feed prices increase 20%+ reducing margins

**Trigger Points:**
- Corn shortage (drought)
- Soybean price increases
- Currency fluctuation
- Transportation costs

**Mitigation Strategies:**
1. **Contract pricing:** Lock in 3-6 month prices
2. **Bulk purchasing:** Buy during harvest (lower prices)
3. **Alternative ingredients:** Sorghum, rice bran
4. **Inventory management:** 2-3 month feed buffer
5. **Supplier relationships:** Multiple suppliers
6. **Forward contracts:** Pre-buy grain

**Response If Occurs:**
- Reduce other costs
- Consider slight price increase
- Delay expansion if needed
- Seek financing for inventory

### 3.3 Cash Flow Crisis

**Description:** Running out of cash before break-even

**Warning Signs:**
- Accounts payable >45 days
- Unable to pay labor
- Skipping feed deliveries
- Maxed credit lines
- Personal funds depleted

**Prevention:**
- 6-month operating reserve
- Diverse revenue streams
- Clear payment terms
- Active collections
- Conservative expansion pace

**Emergency Response:**
1. Immediately cut non-essential expenses
2. Prioritize critical payments (feed, labor)
3. Accelerate collections from customers
4. Negotiate extended payment terms
5. Consider short-term financing
6. Communicate with all stakeholders
7. Pause expansion plans

## 5. Contingency Plans

### Scenario A: 30% Production Loss

**Trigger:** Disease or major mortality

**Actions:**
1. Immediate isolation (days 1-2)
2. SENACSA notification (day 1)
3. Assessment of remaining flock (day 3)
4. Customer notification (day 3)
5. Order fulfillment from backup supply (temporary)
6. Recovery timeline: 3-6 months

**Budget Reserve Needed:** Gs. 15,000,000

### Scenario B: 20% Price Drop

**Trigger:** Market oversupply or competitor pricing war

**Actions:**
1. Assess cost reduction options
2. Focus on highest-margin channels
3. Reduce production costs
4. Negotiate with suppliers
5. Consider value-added products

**Budget Reserve Needed:** Gs. 8,000,000/month for 6 months

### Scenario C: Feed Supply Disruption

**Trigger:** Supplier bankruptcy or transportation strike

**Actions:**
1. Contact alternative suppliers (day 1)
2. Use feed inventory reserves
3. Reduce feeding temporarily if needed
4. Emergency grain purchase
5. Long-term: Multiple supplier contracts

**Inventory Buffer:** 2 months feed supply
